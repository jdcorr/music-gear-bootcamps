# EP–133 (K.O. II) Sampler Bootcamp (14 days) 🔥🎛️

Goal: get fast-fluent on the Teenage Engineering EP–133 K.O. II by building a few coherent projects that cover: sampling workflow, chopping, sample/pad management, sequencing/sync, FX moments, and MIDI/SYNC routing.

END-OF-BOOTCAMP MASTERY ✅
- You can sample quickly (built-in mic vs 3.5 mm input) without getting lost
- You can chop and assign a sample into playable pads/groups
- You can build a groove (drums from samples + at least one melodic layer)
- You can sync and sequence reliably via SYNC/MIDI in/out (and recover from common quirks)
- You can use Punch-in FX™ / distortion / FX moments in a musically intentional way
- You understand the basics of project structure (patterns/bars/scenes) well enough to navigate under pressure

DAILY STRUCTURE ⏱️
- 10 min: warmup checks (project state + sound/pad selection)
- 40–60 min: today’s deliverable practice
- 10 min: “what changed & why” notes (tiny)

COMMON WORKFLOW MUSCLES 🧠
- “Source → Sample → Pad/Group → Sequence → FX moment → Repeat”
- If you feel stuck: back up one step (confirm you’re on the right sample/pad/group before changing timing)
- Keep today’s deliverable small enough to re-record in under ~5 minutes

SAFETY + BATTERY NOTES ⚠️
- Use only new batteries of the same type.
- Insert batteries with correct polarity.
- Don’t mix old/new or mixed chemistry types.
- Don’t dispose of batteries in fire; recycle/dispose per local guidelines.

---

DAY 1: START A PROJECT + FIRST SAMPLE LANE 🧪🎤
Task:
- Power on and create/initialize a clean project.
- Choose a source: built-in mic OR 3.5 mm input.
- Record one short sample (aim for ~2–5 seconds) using the device’s sampling function.
Deliverable:
- One sample that is immediately playable on the pad(s) you expect.

---

DAY 2: SAMPLE DISCIPLINE (LEVELS + CLEAN START) 📏
Task:
- Re-sample the same sound twice.
- First take: conservative (avoid clipping/distortion).
- Second take: a bit hotter but still usable.
Deliverable:
- Two samples with a written note: “which one is easier to chop/sequence and why”.

---

DAY 3: CHOP BASICS (TURN ONE SAMPLE INTO MANY TRIGS) ✂️
Task:
- Take today’s sample and chop it into several meaningful slices.
- Assign resulting slices to pads/groups so you can trigger them rhythmically.
Deliverable:
- One chopped kit: at least 4 pads mapped to different slices.

---

DAY 4: GROOVE DAY (SEQUENCER WITH SLICES) 🥁
Task:
- Create a 4/4 groove using chopped slices.
- Place hits on clear beats and add one variation (e.g., a denser fill or swapped slice for the last bar).
Deliverable:
- A 1–2 bar loop that feels like a real groove (not random triggering).

---

DAY 5: PADS / GROUPS MANAGEMENT 🧩
Task:
- Make one edit pass: adjust which pads use which slices (or groups) to make your kit more intuitive.
- Keep notes on what you changed.
Deliverable:
- Your “v1” pad layout: what each pad means (in 1–3 words per pad).

---

DAY 6: PUNCH-IN FX™ / DISTORTION MOMENT 😈
Task:
- Identify one musical spot (intro pickup OR before a drop).
- Add a single FX moment (Punch-in FX™ and/or distortion/FX, depending on your preferred workflow).
- Keep the rest of the groove stable.
Deliverable:
- One short section where the FX transforms the sound clearly.

---

DAY 7: EXTEND + STRUCTURE (BARS/PATTERNS) 🧱
Task:
- Extend your groove beyond 1 bar.
- Make Phrase A (simple) and Phrase B (variation) using the sequencer’s bar/pattern controls.
Deliverable:
- A 2–4 bar structure that repeats cleanly.

---

DAY 8: ADD A MELODIC LAYER (SAMPLERS AS INSTRUMENTS) 🎼
Task:
- Choose a melodic source (vocal fragment, short tone, or a sample you can pitch/transpose).
- Sample/chop if needed.
- Add a melodic sequence that locks rhythmically to your drum groove.
Deliverable:
- Drums + one melodic line that stays in time.

---

DAY 9: TEMPO / SYNC BASICS (GET IT ROCK-SOLID) ⏱️
Task:
- Practice sync in/out routing:
  - Set tempo so your internal sequencer and external reference agree.
  - Start/stop and confirm the playhead stays coherent.
- If your studio uses external clock: test “record-ready” behavior and recovery after stopping.
Deliverable:
- A synced project you can start and it always lands correctly.

---

DAY 10: MIDI ROUTING CHECK (CHANNEL BEHAVIOR) 🎛️
Task:
- Connect a MIDI controller or external device.
- Verify what data you are sending/receiving (note/voice messages).
- Do a quick test: play a phrase, confirm it triggers the expected pads/instruments.
Deliverable:
- One repeatable “MIDI phrase” you can perform on command.

---

DAY 11: TRIM / EDIT A SAMPLE (FIX THE “ALMOST RIGHT” TAKE) ✂️
Task:
- Pick your least perfect sample.
- Use trimming/edit tools to remove silence or tighten start/end.
- Then update the chops/assignments if needed.
Deliverable:
- One improved sample that’s easier to sequence and sounds tighter.

---

DAY 12: CREATE A MINI TRACK (8–16 BARS) 🧩
Task:
- Assemble an arrangement:
  - Intro (1–2 bars)
  - Main groove (4–8 bars)
  - Variation (1–4 bars)
  - Loop outro (1–2 bars)
- Use FX moment(s) intentionally once you have the groove locked.
Deliverable:
- One complete mini track from your own sampled material.

---

DAY 13: “PERFORMANCE PASS” (MAKE IT RE-PLAYABLE) 🎤
Task:
- Practice performing your track start-to-finish.
- Then improve one bottleneck: sampling-to-sequence speed, pad switching clarity, or FX timing.
Deliverable:
- A version you can start quickly and perform without fumbling.

---

DAY 14: QUALITY PASS + EXPORT INTENT ✅
Task:
- Do a final listening pass.
- Verify: no dead pads, timing feels consistent, transitions are clear, FX moments are purposeful.
- If you export/transfer audio in your workflow, do one end-to-end check.
Deliverable:
- A final “Bootcamp project” plus 3 bullets: what you learned + what you’ll do next.

---

Notes to carry forward:
- This bootcamp intentionally focuses on outcomes (playable pads, coherent grooves, synced start/stop, and intentional FX), not memorizing every menu.
- Use the device’s official EP–133 guide to confirm exact button combos/menu names for your firmware version.
